### 2. Profit & Loss Statement
**What it does:** Shows how profitable your business is
**When to use:** Monthly or quarterly to track performance
**Key info:** Revenue, Costs, Profit, Profit Margins

**How to access:**
1. Go to Reports
2. Click "Profit & Loss" (pink card)
3. Select date range (From/To)
4. Click "Generate Statement"
5. Export to PDF

**What to look for:**
- **Green** = Profitable (good!)
- **Red** = Loss (need to review)
- Check your profit margin % (higher is better)

---

### 3. Balance Sheet
**What it does:** Shows your financial position at a point in time
**When to use:** Quarter-end or year-end
**Key info:** Assets, Liabilities, Equity, Financial Ratios

**How to access:**
1. Go to Reports
2. Click "Balance Sheet" (blue card)
3. Select "As of Date"
4. Click "Generate Balance Sheet"
5. Export to PDF

**Key Ratios:**
- **Current Ratio** > 1.0 = Good liquidity
- **Working Capital** = Assets - Liabilities (positive is good)
- **Debt to Equity** < 1.0 = Good financial health

---

### 4. Cash Flow Statement
**What it does:** Tracks where your cash is coming from and going to
**When to use:** Monthly to monitor liquidity
**Key info:** Cash from customers, Cash to suppliers, Net cash flow

**How to access:**
1. Go to Reports
2. Click "Cash Flow" (teal card)
3. Select date range
4. Click "Generate Statement"
5. Export to PDF

**What to look for:**
- Positive cash flow = âœ… Good
- Negative cash flow = âš ï¸ Monitor closely

---

### 5. Aging Analysis
**What it does:** Shows how old your outstanding receivables/payables are
**When to use:** Weekly/Monthly for collections and payment planning
**Key info:** Outstanding amounts in 30/60/90/120+ day buckets

**How to access:**
1. Go to Reports
2. Click "Aging Analysis" (yellow card)
3. Select type (Receivables or Payables)
4. Select "As of Date"
5. Click "Generate Analysis"
6. Export to PDF

**What to look for:**
- **Red amounts in "Over 120"** = Urgent action needed!
- Focus collections on oldest outstanding amounts
- Plan payments based on vendor aging

---

### 6. Tax Report (GST/VAT)
**What it does:** Calculates your tax liability
**When to use:** Monthly/Quarterly for tax filing
**Key info:** Output tax, Input tax credit, Net tax payable

**How to access:**
1. Go to Reports
2. Click "Tax Report" (orange card)
3. Select date range
4. Click "Generate Report"
5. Export to PDF

**Understanding the report:**
- **Output Tax** = Tax you collected from customers
- **Input Tax** = Tax you paid on purchases (credit)
- **Net Tax** = What you owe (or refund due)

## ðŸ” Troubleshooting

### "Trial Balance doesn't balance"
**Cause:** Data entry errors or missing transactions
**Fix:** Review all recent entries, check for:
- Missing client/vendor transactions
- Incorrect payment allocations
- Data entry mistakes

### "Cash flow shows negative but I have money"
**Explanation:** Cash flow tracks only recorded transactions
**Action:** Ensure all receipts and payments are recorded

### "Aging analysis shows old items"
**Normal:** Outstanding invoices/purchases will show
**Action:** 
- Review and collect on old receivables
- Pay old vendor bills
- Write off bad debts if necessary

### "Tax report shows unexpected amount"
**Check:**
- Tax rates on all invoices
- Purchase tax assumptions (default 18%)
- Date range selected
